Everest Camp Trek

Distance: The trek typically covers around 130 kilometers (80 miles) round trip, starting and ending in Lukla.


Duration: It usually takes about 12-14 days to complete, allowing for proper acclimatization to the high altitude.


Highest Point: The highest point is Kala Patthar at 5,640 meters (18,500 feet).


Difficulty: The trek is considered difficult.


Permits: You’ll need a local government fee (NPR 2,000/US$17 per person) and a Sagarmatha National Park permit (NPR 3,000/US$25 per person) 123.


The journey begins with a flight to Lukla, which has one of the world’s most dangerous airports.


From there, you’ll trek through villages, forests, and beautiful mountains, making memories that will last a lifetime.


Enjoy the breathtaking scenery and the friendly Nepali people.

Walking Holidays

Introductory Walking: If you’re new to walking holidays, consider an introductory trip. These are perfect for a leisurely Sunday afternoon walk. No experience required, but you should be healthy enough to walk 4-5 hours on relatively flat trails.


Moderate Walking: For those who’ve tackled a few hills on Sundays, moderate walking holidays offer a bit more challenge. Expect 5-8 hours of walking per day.


Walking Plus One: Combine walking with another activity or learn a new skill.


Self-Guided Walking: If you prefer independence, opt for self-guided walking holidays. Organized self-guided trips provide maps, notes, accommodation, luggage transfers, and food. No previous experience needed, but map-reading skills help!


Walking Safaris: Experience wildlife by trekking and tracking with expert guides.


A unique way to explore nature and observe animals in their natural habitat.
